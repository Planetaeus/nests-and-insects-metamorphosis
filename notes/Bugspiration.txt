Ants:
	Queens
		Born with wings (lost after mating)
		May have one Queen or multiple Queens depending on species
	Workers
		Females that are unable to mate
		Forage food
		Tend to queen and young
		nest construction
		Defend
		Oldest forage outside
		Younger forage in and near the nest
		Youngest nurse
	Colonies
		Elaborate social structures
		Specially adapted individuals
		feeding
		shelter
		reproduction
	Males
		Reproduce
		After mating, they wander outward and die
	Pheromones
		Trail that lets workers know where food is
		Defense or alarm pheromones send workers into a frenzy
	Competition
		Camouflage - Ball up to look like soil or sand
		Biting or ripping apart
		Mandibles to open a wound, squirt venom in
		Eat the dead, that's good protein.
	Nests
		"Maximum effort"
		Underground nests are horizontal chambers connected by vertical tunnels
		May be just below the surface
		May be created in rotting or dead wood.
		Eggs, larvae, and pupae are moved to warmest part of the nest
		May have been introduced into a human home or forest
	Colony Lifecycle
		Founding Stage
			Nuptial Flights between winged queens and males
			Queens may return to their nests, or found a new one.
		Ergonomic Stage
			Grow the eColony
			Moar Workers, not reproductives
			Can take 1 year, or up to ten years
		Reproductive Stage
			Produce enough reproductives that some heirs will survive and found a new colony
	Feeding
		Trophallaxis
		Workers ingest liquid food and water
		Workers feed solid food to larvae
		Larvae regurgitate solid food as liquid food
		Workers feed liquid food and water to other workers

Termites:
	Soldiers
		Immature males & females primarily focused on defense
		enlarged head w long hard mandibles
		wingless, blind, soft-bodied
		fed by workers
	Neotenics
		under certain conditions, termites may mature sexually
		No wings, no compound eyes, just sex.
		Help increase numbers in the colony.
	Primary Reproductives (King and Queen)
		Swarm together in big flight
		After swarming, lose wings and pair off.
		Colonies can have one queen, several queens, and an undetermined number of kings.
	Workers
		Wingless
		Locate and colonize food
		excavation, repairs
		childcare
		Eat Wood
	Subterranean Termites
		Few adults, most are an immature mix of males and females
		Three castes
			Workers
			Soldiers
			Reproductives
		Mobile nests
			no centralized structure
			move from food source to food source
			often in dead wood to excavate and eat.
	Life Cycle
		Worker Termites
			May remain workers their whole lives
		Soldiers
			Workers molt into pre-soldiers
			pre-soldiers molt into soldiers
		Nymphs
			Worker molts into Nymph with wing buds
			May regress back to a workers
			May molt into Alates with wings
		Neotenic Reproductives
			Secondary come from nymphs, and have wing buds
			Tertiary come from workers, without wing buds.
